Design Process Stages

Select tools from each stage of the UX design process to build a custom UX toolkit*.


In practice the four design stages do not follow any specific order, can occur in parallel, and should be repeated iteratively. As such, each stage should be seen as a different mode that contributes to a project, rather than sequential steps.

A discovery is a preliminary phase in the UX-design process that involves researching the problem space, framing the problem(s) to be solved, and gathering enough evidence and initial direction on what to do next. Discoveries do not involve testing hypotheses or solutions

Define
0

The define phase of a UX design synthesizes learnings from the discovery phase in order to define the goals, objectives, and strategy for the UX. During this phase you detail who your website users are and the requirements they have.

methods
expand_more

Journey Map

One-page visualization of the touchpoints and emotional path a target user segment undergoes to accomplish a goal, with and without a digital application

required work
expand_more

Provide holistic view of the experience, revealing pain points and positive experiences throughout interaction

  • upload_fileDeliverables

    Visual document

  • build_circleTools

    6 to 8 users (over SMEs)

  • scheduleTime Commitment

    8 hrs. per user. Can be divided into multiple smaller sessions across several sprints.

additional info
expand_more

Types

  • Presumptive - how we assume the system is being used by the users.
  • Current State - how we know the system is currently by the users.
  • Prospective State - how we anticipate the system will be used by the users.

Use When

  • Process needs to be visualized, typically immediately after discovery phase
  • Stakeholders may not have a clear idea of the user experience from start to finish in a process

Recommended Pairings

User Interviews, Contextual Inquiry, Empathy Map, occasionally: Stakeholder interview, Service blueprint.

Processes

Build a plausible story for the user to follow

Assumptions

An understanding of the multiple interaction points for the end-user

  • Qualitative personas: captures qualitative data from user research
  • Statistical personas: captures large amounts of quantitative and qualitative data

Ideate / Design
0

Ideation is the process where you generate ideas and solutions through sessions such as Sketching, Prototyping, Brainstorming, Brainwriting, Worst Possible Idea, and a wealth of other ideation techniques. The Design Phase is where UI concepts and visualizations begin to come to fruition, as insights and ideas for your solution start to take on a visual body.

methods
expand_more

Wireframes

A skeletal, colorless depiction of what the product should look like

required work
expand_more

Iterative exploration of the how the layout and information should display on each screen

  • upload_fileDeliverables

    Hand drawn or digital images with accompanying descriptions

  • build_circleTools

    Pen & paper or wireframe software

  • scheduleTime Commitment

    2 hrs/screen

additional info
expand_more

Types

  • Low-fidelity: Simple, rough diagrams for early design conversations
  • Mid-fidelity: More detailed diagrams to differentiate features and content
  • High-fidelity: Detailed enough to explore more complex concepts without distractions of colors or many images

Recommended Pairings

Brainstorming, Contextual Inquiry, Task Analysis, User Flow, prototypes, site map

Resources

A designer, a developer or technical architect and some stakeholder input

Assumptions

There is enough of a product vision established to start organizing information and actions on each screen for the user

Mockups

A more realistic static image of what the product or screen should look like

required work
expand_more

Display and validate what should appear screen by screen prior to development

  • upload_fileDeliverables

    Digital images of devices with wireframe content inserted

  • build_circleTools

    Design software

  • scheduleTime Commitment

    Designers: 2-4 hours per screen
    Client Stakeholders: 1-2 hours to review and provide feedback

additional info
expand_more

Types

  • Low-fidelity: Simple, rough iteration, minimal attention on color, verbiage and visual elements
  • Mid-fidelity: More detailed iteration, more specific relevant verbiage
  • High-fidelity: Detailed enough to be taken as a “final look” of a screen

Use When

Ready to design details of product

Recommended Pairings

Heuristic Evaluation, Design Review, Prototypes, Wireframes,

Resources

Design Software, input of the product vision, user objective

Assumptions

Big picture items have already been discussed

Information Arcitecture

The defining or re-defining of how to structure and organize information within a digital product or service

required work
expand_more

to improve discoverability and/or when restructuring a product or building a new digital product so that the most relevant information is both quick and easy to find. Can improve end-user KPI’s such as time-to-completion, signup, retention, user satisfaction, and more.

  • upload_fileDeliverables

    Site Map, Card Sorting Data, User flow, and/or Navigation Wireframes

  • build_circleTools

    A card sorting software, A software to create user flows and site maps, and 2-8 research participants to be worked with on a 1:1 basis, resources (if needed) to help understand functionality of site, complete inventory of site/product content.

  • scheduleTime Commitment

    From UX Designers: Varies by complexity of product & amount of Information Architecture required
    From Client Stakeholders: 2-4 hours to gather participants for card sorting, understand common use-cases, present site map, and more.

Design Sprint

A design sprint is an intensive method for answering business questions through design, prototyping, and testing with users.

required work
expand_more

Helps you find solutions to high-level, vague, or complex problems and brings clarity to the rest of your process. Short-cuts development & internal debate saving the team time in the long term.

  • upload_fileDeliverables

    Pre-defined design object or product feature(s)

  • build_circleTools

    Sprint master, design sprint team typically no larger than 5-7 members

  • scheduleTime Commitment

    5 days (typically) 40hrs/person

additional info
expand_more

Types

how might we?, lateral thinking, role playing, drawing

Use When

You don’t know what the correct “solution” for your project is. Quick iterative providing real results.

Recommended Pairings

Ethnographic studies, usability testing, journey map, how might we?, brainstorming, matrixes, concept mapping/sketching.

Assumptions

Access to users, time, energy, and willingness to do a deep dive.
