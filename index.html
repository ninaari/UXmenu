Design Process Stages

Select tools from each stage of the UX design process to build a custom UX toolkit*.


In practice the four design stages do not follow any specific order, can occur in parallel, and should be repeated iteratively. As such, each stage should be seen as a different mode that contributes to a project, rather than sequential steps.

Discovery

A discovery is a preliminary phase in the UX-design process that involves researching the problem space, framing the problem(s) to be solved, and gathering enough evidence and initial direction on what to do next. Discoveries do not involve testing hypotheses or solutions

Ideate / Design

Ideation is the process where you generate ideas and solutions through sessions such as Sketching, Prototyping, Brainstorming, Brainwriting, Worst Possible Idea, and a wealth of other ideation techniques. The Design Phase is where UI concepts and visualizations begin to come to fruition, as insights and ideas for your solution start to take on a visual body.
